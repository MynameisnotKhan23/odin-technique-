Lamb Tagine

Description

This Moroccan Lamb Tagine recipe is so tantalizing,it's very known to draw in your neighbors!

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds lamb meat,cut into 1 and a half inch cubes
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil,divided
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • Three-quarters teaspoon garlic powder
  • Three-quarters teaspoon ground coriander
  • One half teaspoon ground cumin
  • One half teaspoon ground cardamom
  • One half teaspoon ground ginger
  • One-quarter teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • One-quarter teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 pinch saffron
  • 2 medium onions,cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 5 carrots,peeled,cut into fourths,then sliced lengthwise into thin strips
  • 3 cloves garlic,minced
  • 1 tablespoon freshly grated ginger
  • 1 lemon,zested
  • 1 can homemade chicken broth or low-sodium canned broth
  • 1 tablespoon sun-dried tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (Optional)
  • 1 tablespoon water (Optional)

Steps

  1. Place lamb and tablespoons olive oil in a large bowl and toss to coat;Set aside
  2. Mix paprika,cinnamon,salt,garlic powder,coriander,cumin,cardamom,ginger,tumeric,cayenne,cloves, and saffron together in a large resealable bag.Add lamb to the bag and toss to coat well.Refrigerate for at least 8 hours,preferably overnight.
  3. Heat remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil in a large,heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat. Add One-third lamb and brown well,5 to 7 minutes. Remove to a plate and repeat to cook remaining two batches of lamb.
  4. Add onions and carrots to the pot and cook for 5 minutes. Stir in garlic and ginger; continue cooking for an additional 5 minutes. Return lamb to the pot and stir in lemon zest, chicken broth, tomato paste, and honey. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer, stirring occasionally, until meat is tender, 1 and a half to 2 hours
  5. If the consistency of tagine is too thin, you may thicken it with cornstarch and water slurry during the last 5 minutes
